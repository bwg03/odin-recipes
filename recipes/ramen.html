Fancy Instant Ramen Upgrade

Description

Ramen may have started as broke-student food, but with a few upgrades, it can pass for a trendy $12 bowl at your favorite late-night spot. This recipe takes your standard instant noodles and gives them a glow-up with soft-boiled eggs, sesame oil, and fresh greens — because you're classy now.

It's still cheap, it still takes like 10 minutes, but now it has depth, complexity, and toppings that scream "I cook." Your microwave ramen just became ramen with intent.

Ingredients

  • 1 pack of instant ramen (any flavor you like)
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 egg
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • A handful of baby spinach or bok choy
  • 1 green onion, sliced
(Optional toppings: soy sauce, chili flakes, sesame seeds)

Steps

  1. Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a small pot.
  2. Gently lower in the egg and boil for 6–7 minutes for a soft-boiled center. Remove and place in cold water to stop cooking.
  3. In the same pot, add ramen noodles and cook according to package instructions.
  4. In the last minute of cooking, stir in spinach or bok choy until just wilted.
  5. Add seasoning packet (or half, for less salt), sesame oil, and stir.
  6. Peel the egg and slice it in half.
  7. Pour noodles and broth into a bowl, top with egg halves, green onion, and any optional toppings.
  8. Slurp with pride.
